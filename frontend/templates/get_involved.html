Get involved

We are actively seeking NHS Trusts and labs who would like to include their data in this project.

In addition, we are always keen to hear from more policymakers, hospital pathologoist, public health staff, funders, microbiologists, GPs, researchers and data scientists!

To express an interest, write to us at ebmdatalab@phc.ox.ac.uk.

Why get involved as a Trust or Lab?

We expect participating regions to see the following benefits:

  • Improved patient care
  • Reduced costs
  • Better documentation and reduction of lab workloads
  • More effective collaboration with GP Practices and commissioners

These benefits will be achieved through:

  • Standardisation of data, supporting benchmarking against other regions
  • Access to a suite of free online tools for supporting audit and feedback, including
    • Ability to identify unnecessary test requests
    • Measures of efficacy, safety and cost-effectiveness presented in easy-to-understand charts for each practice and lab
    • Time trends
    • Automated feedback by email for interested GP practices on unusual testing variation
    • A per-region dashboard showing top-level summary trends and alerts, with national benchmarks
    • Data showing trends over time
  • All the normalised, processed data and analyses will be available as raw data downloads for further analysis within each lab or trust

Ideally, you would allocate some time to provide explanations of known issues and anomalies. We will host occasional meetings in Oxford to discuss development of the tools.

During the prototype period, we are also able to help write software to convert the raw output of a LIMS to this format. Get in touch at hello@ebmdatalab.net to express an interest.

Information Governance

Patient privacy is assured through the University of Oxford's extensive "Privacy by design" protocols. Once our impact assessment has been authorised, we will share it in full here. In short:

  • Data will be submitted to our secured area using best-practice encryption (SSH over a VPN); we will need to liaise with your technical team to set up clients and credentials, following which data uploads will work in the same way, each month.
  • All data sent in this way will remain in a secured server within the University, and processed to ensure it is anonymised and aggregated before it can leave this area.
  • Raw data relating to time periods older than 5 years will be routinely deleted.
  • We will also sign individual data-sharing agreements with each Trust, as required by their data protection protocols.
